This is a math question I got on a test before
I still remember it because the solution is pretty cool.

Draw a circle on a page. First, draw a straight line that passes through the center of the circle (a diameter). Then draw a second straight line at a random location and angle such that every point in the circle is equally likely to lie on this line. What is the probability that the two lines intersect inside the circle?

Alternative wording (by chatgpt): Draw a circle on a page. First, draw a straight line that goes through the center of the circle (a diameter). Next, draw a second straight line at any angle, as long as the line touches the circle. What is the probability that the two lines cross each other inside the circle?

The answer should be in fraction form.
For example, if you believe the answer is 15% then that is 3/20
